  • Prince Vladimir Fyodorovich Odoevsky (1804 - 1869)


For English speakers, the best source of information about Odoevsky is Neil Cornwell’s V.F. Odoevsky His Life, Times and Milieu (pub. 1986 by The Athlone Press). Odoevsky is best known in Russia as the author of the short story for children, the Town in the Snuff box. See a video with the original language here: Городок в табакерке

  • The short story “4338”

    Parts of this story were published in 1835 under the pseudonym “B. Bezglasny” (the name means “Voiceless”), which makes it one of the very earliest works of Science Fiction. It was supposed to become part of a trilogy which would include the historical period of Peter the Great, and also a story from Odoevsky’s own time. Although he planned to do it later in life, Odoevsky never published his complete works, and the fullest version of this story only came out in 1926. As far as I know, the version on this website is the most complete translation of it into English.
